Introduction To SF Crime Data

The crime data has been downloaded from the following source: SF Open Data
Further the data has been cleaned for analysis. The crime data from our visualizations include the following properties:

  • Date is between: 01/01/2014 and 12/31/2016
  • Crime categories are: PROSTITUTION and VEHICLE THEFT

Number of crimes:

  • Total number of crimes from the mentioned period: 22876
  • Number of PROSTITUTION crimes from the mentioned period: 1463
  • Number of VEHICLE THEFT crimes from the mentioned period: 21413

Assignment 2B: Visualizing Geodata

Below is a visualization of prostituion crime data in SF for Year 2014, 2015 and 2016. The circles represents the locations for the occurances within the city.

A K-Means algorithm has been implemented for clustering the occurances of prostitution. The default number of clusters are two (see map). However, it is possible between different number of clusters and view them on the map. As seen on the map, each observations are given a color depending on the cluster it belongs to. Further, the centers of the clusters, also known as centroids, are illustrated as a black bold circle.

In terms of real-world applications, one could imagine the visualisation could help the police departement decide where special units are deployed in the city, depending on the total amount of special units available (amount of clusters).
